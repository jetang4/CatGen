Cat Gene Reference Sheet

A locus is the location of a gene on a chromosome. Different loci are responsible for different traits

Color Loci

The C locus: color

The C locus determines if the cat is colored or not. Full dominance.

  • Dominant: fully colored coat
  • Recessive: albino (no pigment)
  • Special cases: partially colored cats like Siamese or Burmese

Dominant: Fully Colored

Fully pigmented coat

Recessive: No Color

Albino: no pigment in coat

The B locus: black or brown

The B locus determines if the cat is black or brown. Full dominance.

  • Dominant: black
  • Recessive: brown

Dominant: Black

Black Coat

Recessive: Brown

Brown Coat

The D locus: density of color

The D locus determines the density of the cat's coloration

  • Dominant: full color density
  • Recessive: dilute color density

Dominant: Full Color Density

Intense coat color

Recessive: Dilute Color Density

Muted Coat Color

Pattern Loci

The S locus: spotting

The S locus determines if the cat is spotted or solid colored. Incompletely dominant

  • Dominant: at least 50% white
  • Heterozygous: less than 50% white
  • Recessive: fully colored

Dominant: >50% white

More than half of coat is white

Heterozygous: <50% white

The different combinations of alleles at different loci allow cats to have a variety of colors
